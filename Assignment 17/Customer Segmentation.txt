#Perform K-Means clustering on a mall dataset and describe customer groups.

Description:  
The task was to perform K‑Means clustering on a mall dataset and describe customer groups based on their income and spending behavior.

Theory + Example Explanation:

Step 1: Select features such as Annual Income and Spending Score.

Step 2: Apply K‑Means clustering to group customers into segments.

Step 3: Visualize clusters to interpret customer categories.

Typical Customer Groups Identified:

Low Income, Low Spending: Budget‑conscious customers who rarely spend.

High Income, High Spending: Premium customers who are valuable for luxury marketing.

Moderate Income, Variable Spending: Middle‑segment customers with mixed buying behavior.

High Income, Low Spending: Customers with potential but low engagement.

Low Income, High Spending: Customers who spend more despite limited income.

Applied clustering on mall dataset features.
