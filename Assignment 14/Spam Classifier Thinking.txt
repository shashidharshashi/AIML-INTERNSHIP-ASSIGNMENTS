#Design a spam detection system: features, data needed, possible mistakes.

Design Explanation:

1. Features for Spam Detection

Presence of suspicious keywords (e.g., “free,” “win,” “urgent”).

Frequency of links or attachments.

Sender reputation (email domain, IP address).

Message length and unusual formatting (excessive capital letters, symbols).

Historical behavior (previous spam reports).

2. Data Needed

Labeled dataset of emails marked as “spam” or “not spam.”

Metadata such as sender info, subject lines, and timestamps.

Content features like word frequency, n‑grams, and HTML tags.

User feedback data (reports of spam).

3. Possible Mistakes (Challenges)

False Positives: Legitimate emails incorrectly marked as spam (e.g., promotional offers from trusted sources).

False Negatives: Spam emails slipping through undetected.

Bias in Training Data: If dataset is not diverse, classifier may fail on new spam patterns.

Overfitting: Model may learn specific spam examples but fail to generalize.
